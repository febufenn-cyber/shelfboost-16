# Phase 0 Hypotheses

Record each hypothesis before evidence collection. Do not rewrite the hypothesis after seeing results; append the result and decision.

| ID | Hypothesis | Evidence required | Failure signal | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | Stores with 200–2,000 products have material catalog-content debt | Five permissioned audits showing repeated high-priority issues | Problems are rare, intentionally accepted, or low-value | Untested |
| H2 | Small teams leave listings unimproved because manual remediation is expensive | Recent workflow, hours, cost, and backlog examples from eight participants | Work is already cheap, fast, or fully outsourced | Untested |
| H3 | Audit and prioritization are more compelling than an empty AI generator | Participants act on ranked findings and request samples | They only ask for unrestricted generation | Untested |
| H4 | Merchants require preview, factual evidence, per-field approval, history, and rollback | Repeated trust objections and control ranking | Direct auto-publish is broadly accepted | Untested |
| H5 | A structured brand profile plus approved examples improves acceptance | Hybrid profile beats questionnaire-only samples | No measurable acceptance difference | Untested |
| H6 | At least 60% of drafts are accepted unchanged or lightly edited | Three pilots with coded review outcomes | Most drafts need substantial rewrite | Untested |
| H7 | New products, seasonal refreshes, or monitoring create recurring use | Monthly workflow evidence from five buyers | Need is purely one-off | Untested |
| H8 | Qualified buyers will pay at least for a batch or pilot | Payment, deposit, or signed commitment | Interest disappears at commercial ask | Untested |
| H9 | Agencies have stronger frequency and economics than single stores | Five agency interviews and at least one agency pilot | Multi-client needs add complexity without urgency | Untested |
| H10 | A deterministic read-only audit can produce credible first-session value | Findings are understandable and confirmed by merchants | Flags are noisy or obvious without actionability | Untested |

## Confidence scale

- **0 — assumption:** no direct evidence
- **1 — anecdote:** one weak or indirect signal
- **2 — repeated:** several consistent examples
- **3 — operational:** observed behavior or real data
- **4 — commercial:** payment or binding commitment
