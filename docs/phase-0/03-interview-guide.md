# Interview Guide

## Interview rule

Reconstruct a recent workflow before discussing Shelfboost. Specific behavior is evidence; hypothetical enthusiasm is not.

## Opening

> I am researching how Shopify teams manage large product catalogs. I am not selling during this conversation. I want to understand what happened the last time products were added, rewritten, or optimized.

## Workflow reconstruction

1. How many active products and variants do you manage?
2. How many products are added or materially updated each month?
3. Walk me through the last batch from source information to live Shopify page.
4. Who owns product facts, copy, SEO metadata, approval, and Shopify entry?
5. Which files and tools move between those people?
6. How long did the batch take, and where did it wait?
7. Which fields were missing, duplicated, or disputed?
8. What errors reached the live store?
9. What happened to older products after launch?
10. Which part did the team postpone or skip?

## Priority and economics

1. What were the three most painful ecommerce tasks last month?
2. When did you last update more than 20 listings?
3. What happens when 50, 500, or 5,000 products need work?
4. What employee, freelancer, agency, or tool cost is attached?
5. What consequence follows from leaving the backlog untouched?
6. What event makes the work urgent?

## AI experience

1. Have you used ChatGPT, Claude, Shopify Magic, an app, or an agency for this?
2. Show a recent output that was accepted and one that was rejected.
3. Which edits repeat most often?
4. Which mistakes are unacceptable?
5. Which product facts must never be inferred?

## Trust and permissions

Ask the participant to rank each concern from 1 to 5:

- invented facts or claims;
- off-brand voice;
- SEO damage;
- broken HTML or formatting;
- wrong product or variant changed;
- loss of original content;
- broad Shopify permissions;
- private catalog exposure;
- team-member approval mistakes.

Then ask which control would reduce each concern: read-only first, fact source display, diff, per-field approval, low-confidence queue, history, rollback, role approval, or export-only mode.

## Buying and pricing

1. Who owns the budget?
2. What has already been purchased for this job?
3. Is the natural unit a batch, product, store, month, or client portfolio?
4. What result would make a paid pilot obvious?
5. What amount requires additional approval?
6. Would you pay for the next batch now? Why or why not?

## Close

Request one concrete next step:

- permissioned CSV sample;
- live review of five proposed changes;
- paid batch;
- introduction to the actual buyer;
- agency client pilot.

## Post-interview grade

- **A:** real pain, current cost, data supplied, and commercial next step
- **B:** real pain and active workaround; pilot possible
- **C:** interest without urgency or behavior
- **D:** low pain, wrong buyer, or no credible path
