# ICP Scorecard

Use this scorecard before accepting a pilot. Score each factor from 0 to 5 and record evidence, not intuition.

| Factor | Weight | Question |
|---|---:|---|
| Catalog pain | 5 | Are thin, duplicated, missing, or inconsistent fields creating a real backlog? |
| Frequency | 5 | Does the problem recur through launches, imports, seasons, or client work? |
| Current cost | 4 | Is meaningful employee, freelancer, or agency time already spent? |
| Organic importance | 3 | Does search, shopping, or product discovery materially matter? |
| Data readiness | 3 | Can the participant provide a usable CSV or structured product source? |
| Decision access | 3 | Is the interviewee the buyer or close to the budget owner? |
| Trust fit | 3 | Will read-only analysis and controlled review reduce objections? |
| Category safety | 4 | Can copy be improved without high regulated-claims exposure? |
| Reachability | 2 | Can similar buyers be reached repeatedly at low cost? |
| Expansion | 2 | Can one account expand to more products, stores, or recurring work? |

## Interpretation

Calculate `sum(score × weight)`.

- **120+ — priority pilot:** strong fit; seek commercial commitment
- **90–119 — discovery candidate:** interview and inspect data before pilot
- **60–89 — weak fit:** proceed only for a specific learning objective
- **below 60 — decline:** likely distracts from the initial wedge

## Disqualifiers

A high numeric score does not override these early exclusions:

- requests for medical, health, therapeutic, financial, or safety claims without a governed source of truth;
- expectation of immediate autonomous publishing;
- inability to identify the owner of product facts;
- refusal to review samples while demanding guaranteed outcomes;
- request to commit private merchant data to the public repository.

## Candidate comparison

| Candidate | Direct merchant | Agency |
|---|---:|---:|
| Frequency | Medium–high | High |
| Multi-store expansion | Low | High |
| Buying simplicity | Medium | Medium |
| Workflow complexity | Medium | High |
| Claims risk | Category-dependent | Client-dependent |
