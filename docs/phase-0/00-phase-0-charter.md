# Phase 0 Charter

## Mission

Determine whether Shelfboost should advance from a product idea to a deliberately scoped Phase 1 pilot.

Shelfboost advances only when evidence shows that a narrow Shopify segment has a costly, recurring catalog-content problem; the proposed workflow saves meaningful effort; merchants can trust the controls; and at least one buyer pays or makes a concrete commercial commitment.

## Questions Phase 0 must answer

1. Which buyer feels the pain most strongly: founder, ecommerce manager, content team, freelancer, or agency?
2. Which catalog problem creates urgency: thin descriptions, duplicated supplier copy, missing metadata, inconsistent product facts, collection copy, or another job?
3. Is the work recurring enough for a subscription, or is project/batch pricing more natural?
4. Which Shopify fields may be analyzed, drafted, and eventually changed?
5. What proof and controls are required before a merchant permits publishing?
6. Can output preserve product facts while reducing editing time?
7. Can the first customers be reached without uneconomic acquisition?

## Initial segments

### Segment A — direct merchant

Shopify fashion, accessories, home-decor, or lifestyle stores with:

- 200–2,000 active products;
- at least 20 additions or meaningful updates per month;
- no large dedicated catalog-copy team;
- material dependence on organic discovery or shopping feeds;
- lower regulated-claims risk than health, medical, supplement, or baby-safety categories.

### Segment B — agency

Shopify or ecommerce SEO agencies with:

- at least five managed stores;
- repeated catalog audits or product-content work;
- an existing approval process;
- an economic incentive to increase delivery capacity without adding headcount.

## Workstreams

1. Structured interviews
2. Real catalog autopsies
3. Existing-solution analysis
4. Concierge audit and sample-revision pilots
5. Pricing and commitment tests
6. Evidence synthesis and phase decision

## Evidence hierarchy

From strongest to weakest:

1. Paid pilot or deposit
2. Merchant supplies a real export and asks to continue
3. Merchant spends time reviewing drafts and approves them
4. Repeated evidence of current spending or labor
5. Specific recent workflow examples
6. General statements of interest
7. Waitlist sign-up or social engagement

The team must not count weak evidence as if it were payment or operational commitment.

## Success gates

Phase 0 may recommend Phase 1 only when all critical gates pass:

- at least 15 substantive interviews, including at least five agencies or catalog operators;
- at least five real or permissioned catalog samples;
- at least three concierge pilots;
- at least two customers request continued processing;
- at least one paid pilot, deposit, or signed pilot commitment;
- at least 60% of sample revisions accepted unchanged or with light edits;
- factual accuracy is treated as a hard gate, not averaged with style scores;
- one segment and one acquisition path show visibly stronger evidence;
- a recurring job or a viable batch-pricing model is identified.

## Stop or pivot triggers

Pause or change the wedge when:

- catalog content is rarely a top operational pain;
- merchants will not provide data under a read-only, privacy-conscious process;
- most generated drafts require substantial rewriting;
- unsupported claims cannot be controlled reliably;
- the need is one-time and too low-value for viable batch economics;
- no qualified participant will pay despite experiencing the problem;
- agencies and merchants consistently prefer a different field or workflow.

## Data boundary

Real merchant exports, credentials, private analytics, interview recordings, and identifiable notes must remain outside this public repository. Commit only templates, synthetic samples, aggregated findings, or explicitly approved anonymized evidence.

## Definition of done

Phase 0 ends with `09-phase-0-decision.md` completed as one of:

- **CONTINUE** — Phase 1 scope is earned by evidence;
- **PIVOT** — preserve validated learning but change segment, job, pricing, or wedge;
- **PAUSE** — evidence does not justify further build investment.
